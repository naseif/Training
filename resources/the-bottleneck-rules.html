The Bottleneck Rules How to get more done (when working harder isn't working)

Authors published Link
Clarke Ching 2018 Find the book here


Summary

Chapter 1: Introduction

The author wants to teach us how to see bottlenecks.

Chapter 2: Everyday Bottlenecks

The author tells a little story of his way to work one day. Along the way he is showing off some bottlenecks: An airport runway, a coffee machine and the internet connection.

Chapter 3: It isn't obvious

People overlook bottlenecks for various reasons:

  • They do not know that bottlenecks exist.
  • The bottlenecks are devious bastards, so they ignore them.
  • The bottlenecks move around, so they are hard to catch.

Chapter 4: Workplace Bottleneck "Sinead"

The author describes a real life bottleneck in an accounting department and how the problem was identified and removed by just a few questions.

Chapter 5: Egg of columbus

The author tells the story of the egg of columbus making a point that, yes, in hindsight the bottleneck was clear to see and the solution was very clear also. But what is at work here has the name "hindsight bias" and is just our mind playing tricks on us. If it was easy to find and handle bottlenecks we would not have the discussion.

Chapter 6: Workplace Bottleneck "Derek"

The author describes a real life bottleneck in a retail store where the bottleneck has been identified as "the printer" and changing the file format for printing removed the problem. Not buying another printer.

Chapter 7: Wild and Tamed Bottlenecks

The author introduces two classes of bottlenecks.

  • The wild bottleneck
    The wild bottleneck is most often hidden and not managed or poorly managed.
  • The tamed bottleneck
    A tamed bottleneck has a capacity that is too low, but it has been discovered and is now visible and managed.
  • FOCCCUS formula
    The FOCCCUS formula is a rewording of Goldratts 5 steps of focussing and can be used to tame wild bottlenecks.
